The Physics Challenge

Why traditional vertical infrastructure struggles in multi-story developments.

Stack Effect

Thermal Buoyancy

In tall buildings, temperature differences create pressure differentials that fight against roof fans, causing "dead zones" where airflow stagnates on middle floors.

Wind Dam Effect

Horizontal Resistance

External wind pressure on the façade can exceed the rating of small wall fans, causing backdrafts that push odors back into the corridor.

Bio-Film Buildup

Biological Accumulation

Grease and bacteria accumulate in vertical ducts over time, becoming a secondary odor source that is nearly impossible to clean effectively.

The Dual-Action Defense

The synergy of Filtration and Irradiation (Kill & Capture).

1

Surface Irradiation

Ceiling-mounted UVC lamps suppress bacterial growth directly on bins and floors, stopping odors at the source.

2

Particulate Filtration

MERV 6 filters capture dust and aerosols. Crucially, this removes "shielding" particles that would block UV light.

3

Air Sterilization

A dedicated UVC chamber (253.7nm) neutralizes pathogens in the filtered airstream before recirculation or exhaust.

Germicidal Efficiency Curve

Peak efficiency at ~265nm. MSL lamps operate at 253.7nm.

Regulatory Compliance Path

ASHRAE 62.1 Section 6.3

Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) Procedure

Allows for reduced exhaust rates if objective air cleaning is demonstrated. This is the "Alternative Compliance Path" that validates the use of air scrubbers over pure dilution.

ASHRAE 62.1 Section 5.13

Recirculation Limits

Explicitly permits "Recirculation of Class 3 air within the space of origin." This clause legally validates the MSL In-Room Recirculating design for residential floors.
